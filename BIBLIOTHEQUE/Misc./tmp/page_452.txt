8.1, Language

Language is a central feature of human mind-like activity. As noted
in the first chapter, I have stayed away from theorizing about lan-
guage, because | find it formidable in terms of the collection of
results that has accumulated and the degree of controversy that
seems to attend competing views in linguistics. There seems no way
to enter the fray with a little theory, even a highly approximate one.
To do so is to invite the wrath of the linguistic gods. Full blown
from the brow of Zeus or nothing! Clearly, though, any unified
theory of cognition must include a theory of language and language
