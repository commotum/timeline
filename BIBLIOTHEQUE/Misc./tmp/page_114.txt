Another important aspect of problem spaces is the condition that
Operators be freely applicable, that is, that a desired state be ac-
ceptable as the solution to the problem, no matter what operators
were used and in what order, as long as they are operators of the
space. Thus, the states preserve a certain set of properties over the
applications of the operators; it is these properties that are guaran-
teed to hold and do not have to be tested by the test for a desired
state. This free-application condition ties directly to the ability of a
system to search for solutions simply and efficiently. If the exact
sequence of operators were important, then the sequences would
