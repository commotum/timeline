One alternative is that there is some long-term mechanism that
accounts for development. Development takes a long time,
~~years. Thus there could be some undiscovered long-term mech-

~~months. If you take this horn of the dilemma, then your prob-

myelination of nerve fibers (Case, 1985), there are no reasonable
candidates for what might work after the child is a few years old.
You can simply posit something, such as a mechanism for the au-
tonomous growth of short-term memory, but the evidence is not
with you (Chi, 1978). So, that is one alternative.
